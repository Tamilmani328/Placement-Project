1.Hamas Approves Gaza Ceasefire Proposal Hamas has endorsed a new ceasefire plan mediated by Egypt and Qatar. The proposal includes a 60-day truce, the release of hostages, and prisoner exchanges. It comes during a devastating humanitarian crisis in Gaza, where more than 62,000 Palestinians have perished. Protests raging in Israel—drawing around 400,000 participants—target Prime Minister Netanyahu over stalled negotiations. Further, the UK is accelerating efforts to evacuate injured Gazan children for treatment, while global pressure is mounting for sanctions and cessation of the conflict. The Guardian.
2.Trump Rules Out U.S. Ground Troops in Ukraine; Air Support Under Discussion Following talks at the White House, former President Donald Trump confirmed that no American boots will be sent to Ukraine. However, he is open to providing U.S. air support as part of a security framework. Secretary of State Marco Rubio is reportedly leading discussions with U.S., Ukrainian, and European officials to develop a detailed security guarantee plan, potentially formalized by week’s end.
